  • Amalfi

    A quiet and quaint town located at the foot of Monte Cerreto, Amalfi is a haven for thrift shoppers and eaters. Indulge yourself to some of the most exquisite Italian cuisine and the locally famous ?limoncello?. Don't be scared by the sheer size of the lemons. It is what is on the inside that counts. Few other destinations can offer a shopping experience such as Amalfi.

  • Amsterdam

    Amsterdam is the capital and largest city of the Netherlands. It is known for its picturesque canals, historic architecture, and vibrant cultural scene. he city has numerous historical landmarks, such as the Anne Frank House, where Anne Frank and her family hid during World War II.

  • angkor

    Angkor refers to the archaeological site located in the province of Siem Reap, Cambodia. It is one of the most significant historical and cultural landmarks in Southeast Asia. The most famous temple within the Angkor complex is Angkor Wat, often recognized as the largest religious monument in the world. Built in the 12th century by the Khmer Empire.

  • Bali

    Bali is an Indonesian island and province located in the westernmost part of the Lesser Sunda Islands. It is a popular tourist destination known for its stunning beaches, vibrant culture, lush landscapes, and rich spiritual traditions. Bali is renowned for its beautiful beaches. From the popular Kuta Beach with its energetic nightlife to the surf-friendly beaches.

  • Barcelona

    Barcelona is a vibrant city located on the northeastern coast of Spain, in the region of Catalonia. Barcelona is known for its urban beaches. The most popular is Barceloneta Beach, which offers sandy shores, beach bars, and a lively atmosphere. There are also other beaches like Nova Icaria Beach and Bogatell Beach that are slightly quieter and less crowded.

  • British Virigin

    British Virgin Islands (BVI) is a British overseas territory located in the Caribbean. The British Virgin Islands are situated in the northeastern Caribbean Sea, east of Puerto Rico. It is part of the Virgin Islands archipelago. The BVI has several protected areas, including national parks and nature reserves.

  • colosseum

    The Colosseum, also known as the Flavian Amphitheatre, is an iconic ancient Roman monument located in the heart of Rome, Italy. The Colosseum is one of the most iconic symbols of ancient Rome and is considered one of the greatest architectural and engineering achievements of the Roman Empire.

  • Costa Rica

    Costa Rica is a beautiful country located in Central America. Costa Rica is known for its incredible biodiversity. It is home to rainforests, cloud forests, national parks, and wildlife reserves. Visitors can explore diverse ecosystems, spot unique wildlife, and enjoy activities like hiking, bird watching, and canopy tours.

  • Florance

    Florence is a historic city in Italy, famous for its Renaissance art and architecture. Florence is considered the birthplace of the Renaissance and is renowned for its rich art and cultural heritage. The city is home to masterpieces by renowned artists such as Michelangelo, Leonardo da Vinci, and Botticelli.

  • french polynesia

    French Polynesia is an overseas collectivity of France located in the South Pacific Ocean. French Polynesia consists of a group of islands, divided into five archipelagos: Society Islands, Tuamotu Archipelago, Gambier Islands, Marquesas Islands, and Austral Islands. The most well-known islands include Tahiti, Bora Bora, Moorea, and Raiatea.

  • Great Barrier

    The Great Barrier Reef is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is one of the most iconic natural wonders in the world. The Great Barrier Reef is located off the coast of Queensland, Australia, stretching over 2,300 kilometers (1,430 miles). It is the world's largest coral reef system, composed of nearly 3,000 individual coral reefs and more than 900 islands.

  • Iceland

    Iceland, known as the "Land of Fire and Ice," is a Nordic island country located in the North Atlantic Ocean. celand is known for its stunning natural landscapes. It is characterized by volcanic activity, glaciers, geothermal hot springs, waterfalls, and vast open spaces. The island boasts diverse terrains, including lava fields, black sand beaches.

  • Kolkata

    Kolkata, formerly known as Calcutta, is a vibrant and culturally rich city located in the eastern Indian state of West Bengal. Kolkata was the capital of British India until 1911 and played a crucial role in the country's colonial history. The city has witnessed the birth and growth of various nationalist movements and has been a center of intellectual.

  • Machu pichu

    One of the most recently discovered ruins, the Incan ruins of Machu Picchu continue to astound historians, archaeologists and historical enthusiasts alike. A mystery of its real purpose surrounds the place and historians till date continue to debate on the matter. A hotspot within the ruins itself, The Temple of the Sun is known for having windows.

  • Maldives

    The Maldives, a tropical haven of immaculate beaches, are an archipelago of 1,192 coral islands grouped into natural atolls, out of which only a few are inhabited. Time stands still in the Maldives as you lie on the white sand beaches, hearing the crystal clear waves crash against the seashore, swim with the manta rays and reef sharks.

  • New zealand

    New Zealand lies in the Pacific Ocean, to the southwest. New Zealand promises beautiful landscapes and serene, equally beautiful coastlines that you can drive along, listening to the sound of the surf. The country is also known for its adventure sports activities as well as its museums, art galleries and other heritage attractions.

  • New york

    New York City, often dubbed 'The City that Never Sleeps', pulsates with unyielding energy. Its iconic skyline, punctuated by skyscrapers like the Empire State and One World Trade, stands as a beacon of ambition and dreams. The bustling streets, from Broadway's glowing marquees to Central Park's tranquil paths, tell tales of millions. This modern city is home to millions of immigrants and is cosmopolitan in a way no other city can be. Rightly nicknamed 'The city that never sleeps', it is known for its extravagant nightlife - full of music, fashion and life. Words can little describe the unique aura the city radiates and you have to be there to experience it.
